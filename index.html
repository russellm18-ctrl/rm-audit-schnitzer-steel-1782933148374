// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your site renders almost zero indexable content — the crawlable text returns little more than "Radius Recycling" and a cookie consent prompt, meaning every image asset on your domain is a structureless, untagged file with no JSON-LD schema, no descriptive alt-text, and no embedded EXIF location data tying your yards to real geography. You are actively bleeding the query "scrap metal recycling near me" in every metro where you operate physical drop-off and processing locations, handing that traffic to regional operators who have done the bare minimum of geo-tagged image markup. Search engines cannot distinguish your ferrous and nonferrous processing operations from a placeholder page.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Industrial procurement coordinators and demolition contractors — buyers moving hundreds of tons and needing verified operational scale before they make a call — land on your domain and find no visual confirmation that your facilities can handle their volume. No yard footage, no active processing imagery, no visible proof of your auto shredder infrastructure or baling capacity means your written capability claims are completely unsupported, and that buyer exits to a competitor whose site shows the actual equipment. In B2B scrap, trust is earned by showing the machine, not describing it.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Regional scrap yards operating a fraction of your volume are publishing geo-tagged yard walkthroughs, time-stamped scale house imagery, and current material acceptance boards directly to Google Business Profile — assets that earn local map pack placement and answer the contractor's pre-call verification checklist visually. You have multi-state infrastructure across the Pacific Northwest and beyond, and none of that operational depth is visible in search-adjacent image assets, which means a single-yard independent with a smartphone and a consistent upload cadence is outranking your physical locations on the exact queries your tonnage volume should dominate. That gap is not a branding problem — it is a structured data absence costing you inbound commercial accounts.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a facility-by-facility visual capture protocol across your active yards — scale houses, shredder lines, material sorting floors, vehicle entry points, and container staging areas — and deploy every image with location-specific alt-text, structured JSON-LD markup identifying each asset as a local business with service-area and material-type schema, and EXIF coordinates locked to each yard's GPS address before a single file is uploaded. Each image becomes a discrete, crawlable data point that connects a search query to a specific Schnitzer location and a specific accepted material category — ferrous, nonferrous, catalytic converters, end-of-life vehicles — rather than a decorative file serving no indexing function. This is not a photoshoot recommendation; it is a location-by-location data deployment that converts your existing physical infrastructure into a structured, search-visible asset network.
